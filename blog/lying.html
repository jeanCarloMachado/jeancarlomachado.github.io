Lying

July 24, 2018

This is a summary/paraphrase with some complements from the small yet resourceful book Lying from Ham Harris. I’m a big fan of Sam and this book is a piece of extremely useful philosophical argument that each one of us can upload to our operational systems and enjoy a better life. Given that I thought was reasonable to write some lines about it and share here.

Most people already believe that lying is wrong. Yet most of us do it all the time. At least 1 study suggest that 10% of communication between spouses is deceptive. 38% of encounters among collage students contain lies. We may even think they are doing good when lying out of compassion for others also known as white lies. Research suggest that all lies incur in less satisfactory relationships. Even liars rate their deceptive interactions as less pleasurable than honest one’s. Why do we deliberately use a reliable source of suffering?

We may think it is moral to tell white lies. But by lying we deny the receiver access to reality. And the resulting ignorance often harms them in ways we did not anticipate. Our friends may act on our falsehoods. Or fail to solve problems they could have solved only on basis of good information. Rather often to lie is to infringe in the freedom of those we care about. The more a person well being depends of a correct understanding of the world the more the consequential the lie.

When a woman asks if she looks fat in her dress many people might think that there’s only one reply possible: no. But if she really is and you say no you may be preventing her to get honest information to be able to act on a problem that is harming her life. She might deeply desire to find a husband and by deceiving her you might be making her life harder.

To lie is to intentionally deceive others when they are expecting honest communication. You can be truthful yet wrong. But communication your level of degree in each interaction is a signal of honesty. In believing one thing and indenting to communicate another is the essence of lying.

When we lie we assume that the other person is dangerous or incapable to handle the truth. Sincerity, authenticity, integrity and mutual understanding are destroyed the moment we deliberately misrepresent our believes Deeper relations possibilities are lost.

In the book Sam recalls the case where a mother, upon visiting the doctor is discovered to have cancer. The doctor don’t tell her directly, instead tells her husband which decides not to tell her at all. After some months she goes to the library and diagnose herself. At some point the situation worsens and she dies in the hospital bed. When finally the hole family faces the situation. They spent all the time pretending. What conversation they might have otherwise in order to prepare one another for that moment and the future? What words that mother might have said to their children on her last days if she was not committed to deception? They end up better of with hiding?

Honesty can also force any dysfunction in your life to the surface. As Tim Ferris once said “A person’s success in life can usually be measured by the number of uncomfortable conversations he or she is willing to have.” When you confront the truth at every moment you may find that some relationships cannot be honestly maintained. That the way you’re heading your life will lead to lesser well being than otherwise. An effort to tell the truth may reveal truths about oneself.

In committing to be honest with everyone we commit to avoid a wide range of long-term problems, at the cost of short-term discomfort. The discomfort should not be exaggerated: The purpose of telling the truth is not offend people. You simply want them to have the information you have.

When we tell lies we are usually loosing the opportunity to form deeper bonds. Honesty is a gift we can give others. And honest people are a refuge. With them, you know you will get honest feedback and praise cannot be mistaken by mere flattery. Lying is precisely the sort of behaviour we need to outgrow in order to build a better world.
